A survival-platformer that explores schizophrenia through a fragmented mindscape where medication, routine, and perception become tools of survival. The project focuses on creating visual tension, emotional immersion, and awareness of mental health through minimalist interaction and atmospheric storytelling.

Degree project

ROLE

I was in charge of designing the user interface and key visual elements of the game, including screens such as gameplay, pause, and settings.

Also I developed the visual concept for the enemies, designed as hallucinations based on research into schizophrenia. All assets were created under the supervision of a project lead, who reviewed and approved each element.

This mobile test is a fan-made interactive experience inspired by Hazbin Hotel, the animated series by Vivziepop, now streaming on Amazon. This is a purely entertainment-focused project with no psychological or diagnostic basis, intended to engage fans and celebrate the series’ music, characters, and community.

Degree project

ROLE

I was fully in charge of the visual and UI design of the test, carefully crafting each screen to reflect the unique aesthetic of Hazbin Hotel. From typography and color palette to icons and transitions, every design decision was guided by the show’s bold, chaotic, and theatrical visual identity. The goal was to create an immersive fan experience that felt consistent with the world of the series while remaining intuitive and engaging for mobile users.
